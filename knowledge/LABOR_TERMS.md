# Labor Law Glossary

Key terms every union steward should know.

---

## A

**Arbitration**
A process where a neutral third party (arbitrator) hears both sides and makes a binding decision. Usually the final step in the grievance procedure.

**At-Will Employment**
Employment that can be ended by either party at any time for any reason. Union contracts replace at-will with just cause protection.

---

## B

**Back Pay**
Wages an employee would have earned if not for an improper action. A common remedy in grievances.

**Bargaining Unit**
The group of employees represented by a union, defined by job classifications and/or work locations.

**Bumping Rights**
The right of a senior employee to displace a junior employee during layoffs, usually into a position they previously held or are qualified for.

---

## C

**CBA (Collective Bargaining Agreement)**
The contract between the union and employer that governs wages, hours, and working conditions. Also called "the contract."

**Caucus**
A private meeting between the union team (or management team) during negotiations or grievance meetings.

**Classification**
A job category defined in the contract, with specific duties and pay rates.

**Continuing Violation**
A violation that occurs repeatedly over time. Each occurrence restarts the time limit for filing a grievance.

---

## D

**Discipline**
Corrective action taken against an employee, including verbal warnings, written warnings, suspensions, and termination.

**Disparate Treatment**
Treating employees differently for the same offense without legitimate justification. A common basis for grievances.

**Due Process**
The right to fair treatment, including notice of charges, opportunity to respond, and impartial decision-making.

**Duty of Fair Representation (DFR)**
The union's legal obligation to represent all bargaining unit members fairly, without discrimination or arbitrary treatment.

---

## E

**Excelsior List**
A list of employee names and addresses that employers must provide to unions before representation elections.

**Exclusive Representative**
The certified union that has the sole right to represent all employees in the bargaining unit for collective bargaining purposes.

---

## F

**Final and Binding**
A term describing arbitration decisions that cannot be appealed (with limited exceptions).

**FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act)**
Federal law providing up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave for qualifying medical and family reasons.

---

## G

**Good Faith Bargaining**
The legal requirement for both parties to negotiate honestly and with intent to reach agreement. Neither side can simply go through the motions.

**Grievance**
A formal complaint that the contract or past practice has been violated. The process for resolving disputes.

**Grievant**
The employee who files or is the subject of a grievance.

---

## H

**Hearing**
A formal proceeding where evidence is presented and witnesses testify, typically before an arbitrator.

---

## I

**Impasse**
When negotiations reach a standstill and neither party will move. May lead to mediation or other dispute resolution.

**Information Request**
A formal union request for information from the employer needed to investigate or process a grievance. Employers have a duty to provide relevant information.

---

## J

**Just Cause**
The standard requiring employers to have legitimate, fair reasons for discipline. Tested by the Seven Tests of Just Cause.

---

## L

**Layoff**
Temporary or permanent separation from employment due to lack of work, not employee misconduct.

**Lockout**
When an employer prevents employees from working during a labor dispute.

---

## M

**Make Whole**
A remedy that restores an employee to the position they would have been in without the violation, including back pay and benefits.

**Management Rights**
Powers retained by the employer, often listed in the contract. Limited by other contract provisions.

**Mediation**
A voluntary process where a neutral third party helps the parties reach agreement. Unlike arbitration, the mediator has no power to impose a decision.

**Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)**
An agreement between parties, often supplementing the main contract.

---

## N

**NLRA (National Labor Relations Act)**
The federal law that gives private sector employees the right to organize, bargain collectively, and engage in concerted activity.

**NLRB (National Labor Relations Board)**
The federal agency that enforces the NLRA and conducts union elections.

---

## O

**Overtime Equalization**
Contract provisions requiring overtime to be distributed fairly among qualified employees, often tracked on a list.

---

## P

**Past Practice**
An established pattern of behavior or interpretation that becomes binding on both parties, even if not written in the contract.

**Precedent**
Prior decisions (especially arbitration awards) that may guide future cases with similar facts.

**Progressive Discipline**
A system of escalating consequences (warning, written warning, suspension, termination) designed to correct behavior.

---

## R

**Recall Rights**
The right of laid-off employees to be called back to work, usually in seniority order.

**Recognition Clause**
The contract section that defines which employees the union represents.

**Remedy**
The solution or relief requested in a grievance, such as reinstatement, back pay, or policy change.

---

## S

**Seniority**
Length of service, used to determine rights to promotions, layoffs, vacation scheduling, and other benefits.

**Settlement**
An agreement resolving a grievance before arbitration. May include partial remedies.

**Steward**
A union representative in the workplace who handles grievances and represents members.

**Strike**
A work stoppage by employees to pressure the employer during a labor dispute.

**Subcontracting**
Having work performed by outside contractors instead of bargaining unit employees.

**Sunset Clause**
A provision removing old discipline from an employee's record after a specified time period.

---

## T

**Time Limits**
Deadlines for filing grievances and responses at each step. Often strictly enforced.

---

## U

**ULP (Unfair Labor Practice)**
A violation of the NLRA, such as interfering with organizing or refusing to bargain in good faith. Filed with the NLRB.

**Union Security**
Contract provisions requiring employees to pay dues or fees as a condition of employment (where legal).

---

## W

**Weingarten Rights**
The right of union-represented employees to have a steward present during investigatory interviews that could lead to discipline.

**Work to Rule**
A job action where employees do only exactly what the contract requires, following every rule precisely. Legal alternative to striking.

---

## Z

**Zipper Clause**
A contract provision stating that the written agreement is complete and neither party can demand to bargain over other issues during the contract term.
