# Grievance Types and Defense Strategies

This guide covers the most common types of grievances and how to approach defending each one.

---

## Discipline Grievances

### Termination/Discharge

**What it is:** Employee fired from their job

**Key questions:**
- Was just cause met? (All 7 tests)
- Was progressive discipline followed?
- What's the employee's record?
- Were others treated the same?

**Common defenses:**
- Lack of progressive discipline
- Disparate treatment
- Mitigating circumstances
- Procedural violations
- Insufficient evidence

**Best evidence to gather:**
- Personnel file
- Past discipline records
- Comparable cases
- Witness statements
- Work history/performance reviews

---

### Suspension

**What it is:** Employee sent home without pay

**Key questions:**
- Is suspension appropriate for this offense?
- Was investigation completed first?
- How long is reasonable?

**Common defenses:**
- Punishment too severe
- No prior discipline
- Suspension used as punishment before investigation
- Length excessive for offense

---

### Written Warning

**What it is:** Formal documented warning

**Key questions:**
- Is the warning accurate?
- Was employee given chance to respond?
- Will it affect future discipline?

**Common defenses:**
- Factual inaccuracies
- No opportunity to respond
- Retaliation for protected activity
- Inconsistent application

---

## Contract Violation Grievances

### Overtime Violations

**What it is:** Overtime not distributed fairly or paid correctly

**Key questions:**
- What does the contract say about OT distribution?
- Is there an equalization clause?
- Was the right person bypassed?

**Common defenses:**
- Contract language is clear
- Past practice supports grievant
- Overtime records prove disparity

**Best evidence:**
- Overtime records/lists
- Schedules
- Contract language
- Past practice examples

---

### Seniority Violations

**What it is:** Seniority not respected in job assignments, layoffs, recalls

**Key questions:**
- What does seniority apply to per contract?
- Is ability a factor?
- Was the process followed?

**Common defenses:**
- Clear contract violation
- Senior employee was qualified
- No legitimate reason to bypass

---

### Scheduling Violations

**What it is:** Improper scheduling, shift assignments, or changes

**Key questions:**
- What notice is required?
- Who has preference?
- Is there premium pay owed?

**Common defenses:**
- Insufficient notice given
- Contract procedures not followed
- Make-whole remedy required

---

### Subcontracting

**What it is:** Work given to outside contractors

**Key questions:**
- Does contract address subcontracting?
- Were bargaining unit employees available?
- Was the union notified?

**Common defenses:**
- Work historically done by unit
- Qualified employees available
- No notification to union
- Violation of recognition clause

---

## Pay and Benefits Grievances

### Wage Disputes

**What it is:** Incorrect pay, missing pay, wrong rate

**Key questions:**
- What should the rate be?
- How long has error occurred?
- What's the total owed?

**Common defenses:**
- Contract specifies correct rate
- Calculate full back pay
- Include interest if applicable

---

### Benefit Denials

**What it is:** Health insurance, pension, leave denied

**Key questions:**
- What does contract/policy say?
- Did employee meet requirements?
- Was denial timely?

**Common defenses:**
- Employee met all requirements
- Policy not followed
- Similar situations handled differently

---

### Holiday Pay

**What it is:** Holiday pay denied or calculated wrong

**Key questions:**
- Was employee eligible?
- Did they work required days?
- Is the rate correct?

**Common defenses:**
- Contract eligibility met
- Past practice shows payment
- Calculation error documented

---

## Working Conditions Grievances

### Safety and Health

**What it is:** Unsafe working conditions

**Key questions:**
- Is there a hazard?
- Was it reported?
- What does OSHA say?

**Common defenses:**
- Right to safe workplace
- Employer knew of hazard
- Corrective action required

---

### Harassment

**What it is:** Hostile work environment, bullying

**Key questions:**
- Is conduct documented?
- Was it reported?
- What action was taken?

**Common defenses:**
- Pattern of behavior
- Employer failed to act
- Contract anti-harassment language

---

### Unreasonable Work Assignments

**What it is:** Excessive workload, duties outside classification

**Key questions:**
- What's in the job description?
- Is workload safe?
- Are others doing less?

**Common defenses:**
- Out of classification work
- Safety concerns
- Past practice on assignments

---

## Procedural Grievances

### Denial of Representation

**What it is:** Weingarten rights violated

**Key questions:**
- Did employee request rep?
- Was request denied?
- Did interview continue?

**Common defenses:**
- Clear request documented
- Interview proceeded anyway
- Discipline should be voided

---

### Information Requests

**What it is:** Employer refusing to provide information

**Key questions:**
- Is information relevant?
- Was request reasonable?
- How long have they delayed?

**Common defenses:**
- Information needed for representation
- Duty to provide information
- Delay prejudices grievance

---

### Timeliness Issues

**What it is:** Grievance or response untimely

**Key questions:**
- What are the time limits?
- When did clock start?
- Are there exceptions?

**Common defenses:**
- Continuing violation doctrine
- Employer waived timeliness
- Discovery rule applies

---

## Quick Reference: Defense Checklist

For ANY grievance, always check:

| Item | Question |
|------|----------|
| Contract | What does the contract say? |
| Past Practice | How has this been handled before? |
| Disparate Treatment | Were others treated differently? |
| Procedure | Were proper steps followed? |
| Evidence | What proof exists? |
| Witnesses | Who saw what happened? |
| Timeline | When did everything occur? |
| Remedy | What does the employee want? |
